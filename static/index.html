Why sign language matters

Hands are the voice of the deaf.

I

Inclusion

Sign language is a full, natural language. Using it helps include Deaf and mute people in conversation, education, and work.

L

Learning

Families and friends can learn signs to communicate with loved ones. Kids can practise letters and numbers in a fun way.

V

Voice

See the sign you make, get it as text, hear it spoken in English, Hindi, or Marathi, and feel braille-style vibrations.

How it works

  1. 1 Open the Detector tab and allow camera access.
  2. 2 Show one or two hands — letters A–Z, numbers 0–9, or words like Hello, Thank you.
  3. 3 The sign appears in the output box; use Next word to speak it and start the next.

Sign reference (ASL / ISL)
